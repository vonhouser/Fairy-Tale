Why Alice Could Kick Cinderella's Ass

Alice in Wonderland and Cindarella are two popular children's stories. While Cindarella was originally a Grimm's Fairytale, the version I am referring to here is the one made popular by Disney. I wanted to analyze the difference between these two popular female characters in terms of some positive qualities such as strength and courage, as well as the interesting plot elements such as Alice's use of drugs and Cindarella's insessant need to be rescused, first by the Fairy Godmother, and then by the Prince.

The data are based on asking participants (me) How they would rank the main character of the story on ten key factors at five points during the story. The questions used for the scale were based on extended Likert scores. For example, participants (me) were asked "How would you rank Alice on her level of courage at this point in the story, on a scale from 1 to 10?" or "How would you rank the number of hallucinogens apparently consumed at this point, with 1 being 'None', and 10 being 'Jefferson Airplane Might Write a Song About It', up to this point in the story?"

It seems clear from these findings that based on her willingess to go down the rabbit hole, to argue with strange talking animals, to drink unkwown substances, and to look to no one but herself for rescue, even under threat of beheading, Alice could clearly kick Cinderella's ass. Even the new Drew Barrymore version.
